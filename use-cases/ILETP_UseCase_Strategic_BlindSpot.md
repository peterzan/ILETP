# Use Case: The Strategic Blind Spot
## Why AI Public Benefit Corporations Should Champion ILETP™ (But Aren't Yet)

---
## Executive Summary

The AI ecosystem is missing a foundational layer of accountability.
Leading labs are focused on bigger models, better routing, safety protocols, fine tuning pipelines, and vertical integrations but none of that resolves the core issue: **no model can hold itself accountable, and “multi-model” solutions still lack independent verification**.

The result is a structural blind spot: the organizations most committed to safety and public benefit have not yet realized that the missing infrastructure is **a trust fabric between models**, not new models or new policies. And because this blind spot exists at the institutional level, it propagates into the design of their systems, their product direction, their governance structures, and their long-term strategies.

ILETP fills this gap by operationalizing **trustworthiness through accountability**, formalizing independent verification, divergence analysis, provenance, and cross-model consensus. This is not an alternative to current AI roadmaps; it is the infrastructure those roadmaps are silently assuming but have not yet built.

The window to define this layer is open for 18–36 months. If the mission aligned organizations don’t establish this standard, others will and less aligned actors will shape its direction. By adopting or supporting ILETP as an open, neutral, sovereignty-respecting trust layer, labs, developers, enterprises, and regulators gain a common foundation for safe, reliable, globally interoperable AI systems.

This isn’t about selling a product or promoting a vendor.  It’s about recognizing that **accountability infrastructure is the missing layer in AI**, and ILETP offers a credible, open-source path to build it now, before the standards lock in.

You're already structured as a Public Benefit Corporation or mission aligned entity. You already invest heavily in AI safety and trust. You already talk about democratizing access and building responsibly.
